Never been to a concert and have future plans to go on one? Allow me to provide prior information about what might happen on the d-day of a concert.

But first, let me share my own concert experiences from the first time I had to go alone until the third time.

February 25-26 this year they came back to the Philippines as one of the stops in the third leg of their world tour, the EXOr'dium. Unlike the first time, I know very well how concerts work before, during, and after. I also had someone to go with for both days, yes, this time, I attended both days.



Hanna and I went to Araneta Coliseum on the first day as early as 10am to claim our bundles, orders as well as take part in various giveaways from different local fanbases. I never knew it will be that fun even if we were walking and running from place to place under the heat of the sun.

Given that I have enough experience on concerts, let me share some of the dos and don'ts on d-day:

Disclaimer: I do not know if the same goes with western artists but based on my experience, almost all Korean acts have the same scenarios.

  • Prepare all the necessary things you need to bring a day or two before the concert day (i.e. concert ticket, power bank, lightstick, pocket money for merchs)
  • Always behave properly especially when queuing for giveaways because I often experience that while queuing, securities get mad that they asks us to leave the place.
  • When the concert starts, WAVE YOUR LIGHTSTICK, SING YOUR FANGIRL HEART OUT, AND CHEER FOR THEM.
  • DO NOT bring dangerous weapons as it will also be inspected as you enter the arena.
  • DO NOT push people while queuing in the line. All of you are the same, all of you are just fans, no one is superior nor inferior.
  • AVOID raising banners, lighsticks and cellphones when the group is performing because it will hinder the fans at the back from watching.
  • ALWAYS respect each other especially when you are in the pit section.

    • AND,

  • BE EXTRA CAREFUL AND MINDFUL. Take care of your belongings and yourself as well. I have encountered fansites who actually harm other fans just to take photos of the boys.

AND, one more thing, think of every concert experience as your first and make the most out of every moment.
